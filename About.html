LINGAP: Linking Indigenous Narratives for Greater Academic Preservation

About LINGAP

Problem:

The Philippines' linguistic diversity is under peril, with several dialects threatening extinction due to Tagalog's dominance as the primary language. Minority dialects are marginalized, which leads to a lack of documentation and resources for their preservation. Without persistent effort, these distinct linguistic terms, like the cultural heritage they represent, risk falling into obscurity.

Solution:

Our startup aims to address this gap by developing a comprehensive platform for documenting and preserving Philippine dialects. Users can contribute knowledge and thoughts about their individual dialects via interactive forums, creating a collaborative platform for linguistic exchange. Using community-generated information, we will create a dynamic e-library/archive with a tagging system for simple navigation by dialect and area. Furthermore, our new map function will show visitors a visual depiction of dialect distribution, raising awareness and knowledge of linguistic variety in the Philippines.

Business Model:

To support our purpose, our business will operate as a non-profit organization, relying on donations from individuals, organizations, and institutions who share our dedication to language preservation. In addition, we plan to form relationships with academic institutions, government agencies, and cultural organizations to broaden our reach and effect.

Call to Action:

We invite dedicated individuals, linguists, educators, and cultural lovers to work with us on this ground-breaking project. Whether you are skilled in linguistics, technology, community outreach, or fundraising, your contributions can make a major difference in our efforts to preserve Philippine dialects for future generations. Furthermore, we encourage potential donors who appreciate the importance of preserving linguistic diversity and would like to financially support our work. Together, we can ensure that the diverse fabric of Philippine languages continues to exist and grow.
